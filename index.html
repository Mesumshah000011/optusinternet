How Does Optus Internet Compare to Tangerine Internet for Home Use?

Torn between Optus internet and Tangerine internet? You're definitely not the only one. These two names are everywhere in Australia's broadband market. They're both solid, but when it comes to picking one for your home, things can get a bit tricky.

No fluff. No complicated terms. Let's just keep it real and simple.

What's Good About Optus Internet?

Network and Coverage

Optus owns its network. It offers NBN, mobile broadband, and 5G home internet. If you're in or near the city, chances are you'll enjoy fast speeds and good reliability.

Plans and Pricing

Optus gives you three options for home use:

  • NBN (from Basic to Ultra-Fast)
  • 4G/5G Home Internet
  • Mobile Broadband

Prices are a bit on the higher side. But you get a modem included and sometimes even entertainment perks like Netflix or Fetch.

Customer Support

You can call, chat, or use their app—day or night. Support is always on. They've been around long enough to know how to keep things working.

What's Great About Tangerine Internet?

Network and Speed

Tangerine doesn't have its own network. It uses the NBN. But don't worry—it still offers good speeds across every plan. You won't notice much difference unless you're measuring every single millisecond.

Plans and Affordability

Tangerine internet is all about saving money. Their plans are cheaper—especially for the first six months. Here's what they offer:

  • No lock-in contracts
  • A 14-day trial to test the waters
  • Option to bring your own modem or buy one

They've got plans from NBN25 to NBN1000. Enough for most people.

Customer Support

Support is available seven days a week. It's not round-the-clock, but their team usually responds fast. Most users are happy with the help they get.

Which One Is Cheaper?

Let's look at the popular NBN50 plan:

  • Optus NBN50: Around $79/month. Modem included. No setup fees.
  • Tangerine NBN50: $59.90/month for the first six months. Then $69.90/month. BYO modem or buy one.

Tangerine internet clearly wins on price. But if you want more than just basic internet, Optus internet brings some extra perks to the table.

Modem and Setup: What to Expect?

Optus

  • Free modem with most plans
  • 4G backup modem included
  • Easy setup, just plug it in

Tangerine

  • Bring your own modem or buy one for $129.90
  • Setup is DIY, but simple
  • No setup charges

If you want something that works straight out of the box, go with Optus. Tangerine is fine too, but you'll need to be a bit hands-on.

Extras and Add-Ons

Optus internet gives you more than just connection:

  • Add-ons like Netflix, Amazon Prime, Fetch TV
  • Free Optus Sport and OS fitness
  • Discounts on mobile plans

Tangerine internet keeps it simple. No extras. Just reliable internet.

If you want entertainment built into your plan, Optus wins this round.

Contracts and Flexibility

Both let you go contract-free. But there are a few details:

  • With Optus, if you leave early on a 24-month plan, they might charge for the modem.
  • With Tangerine, there's no catch. You can cancel whenever.

Tangerine also gives you a 14-day trial. So if it's not working for you, no harm done.

Best for Working From Home?

Optus

  • Strong and stable speeds
  • Backup options if NBN goes down

Tangerine

  • Speeds are good for most work
  • Great for anyone watching the budget

If your job depends on staying connected 24/7, Optus is the safer pick. But for general Zoom calls and emails, Tangerine is just fine.

Better for Gaming?

Gaming needs more than speed. You want:

  • Low ping
  • Consistent connection
  • Enough bandwidth for downloads and live play

Optus tends to handle peak hours better and usually offers slightly lower latency. Still, if you're in a city, Tangerine can hold up well.

For serious gamers, Optus is worth it. If you're more casual, Tangerine works.

What Should You Go With?

Choose Optus internet if:

  • You want extra features
  • You want a modem included
  • You like streaming bundles and entertainment add-ons

Pick Tangerine internet if:

  • You're on a budget
  • You want true flexibility
  • You're okay with fewer extras

Still Can't Decide?

Try Tangerine first. You get a 14-day trial. If it doesn't work for you, cancel and switch to Optus. No stress.

Both are reliable. It really comes down to what matters more—extra features or monthly savings.

Final Thoughts

Picking between Optus internet and Tangerine internet isn't about which is best overall. It's about what fits your needs. Want solid internet with bonus extras? Go Optus. Want something affordable and simple? Tangerine is a smart choice.

FAQs

Q: Does Tangerine internet include a modem?

No. You can bring your own or buy one through them.

Q: Is Optus internet better for streaming?

Yes, especially with the entertainment add-ons and faster peak-hour performance.

Q: Can I cancel Tangerine anytime?

Yes. Their plans are flexible with no lock-in contract.

Q: Does Optus charge for the modem?

Not always. But if you're on a contract and leave early, you might have to pay for it.

Q: Which is cheaper—Optus or Tangerine?

Tangerine is cheaper, especially for the first six months.
